The Growth of Cities

The rise of megacities across the globe has transformed human society. In 1950, only New York and Tokyo boasted populations over 10 million. Today, there are dozens of such urban centers spread across every continent.

While urbanization drives economic growth and technological innovation, it also presents severe environmental challenges. Air pollution, inadequate waste management, and water scarcity are common issues threatening the quality of life in these massive hubs.

Experts suggest that the future of cities relies heavily on smart infrastructure. Investing in renewable energy and green public transport could potentially mitigate the ecological footprint of urban sprawl.
